The Mechanism

1. Marbles arranged along the hypotenuse (slope). At rest.

2. Gravity pulls them down. They accelerate toward the ground.

3. Ground contact stops downward motion. Forces redirect sideways through collisions.

4. Momentum redistributes outward — the dispersion explosion.

Key Equations

Collision Impulse:

j = -(1+e)(v_rel · n) / (1/m₁ + 1/m₂)

Velocity Update:

v₁′ = v₁ + (j/m₁)n

v₂′ = v₂ - (j/m₂)n

Dispersion:

D(t) = (1/N) Σ ||x_i - x_cm||

Key Insight

When marbles hit the ground, vertical motion is constrained. Momentum redistributes laterally through collisions, causing rapid outward dispersion.

This is the transition from ordered motion to chaotic redistribution.
