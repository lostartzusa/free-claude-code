How It Works

All primes > 5 avoid multiples of 2, 3, and 5. The survivors follow a repeating pattern with gaps of +2, +4, +2, +10, +2, +10 (sum = 30).

Twin primes are pairs that differ by exactly 2 (e.g., 11&13, 17&19, 29&31).

The Twin Prime Conjecture says there are infinitely many twin prime pairs. Unproven since 1849.

This matrix shows every candidate position where twin primes could occur — and which ones actually are prime.

The Pattern

Each row covers 30 consecutive integers.

The 6 candidates per row at offsets +0, +2, +6, +8, +18, +20 from the row start are the only positions where primes can appear.

Twin primes can only occur at the +2 gaps — positions (0,2), (6,8), and (18,20) within each row of 30.

That's 3 possible twin prime slots per 30 numbers. The conjecture: do they keep appearing forever?
